# Hydrion Alpha and Beta Testing Notice

**Effective date:** July 6, 2026  
**Last updated:** July 6, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0.0

Hydrion builds may be distributed as MVP, alpha, beta, preview, internal, or test builds before stable public release. Testing builds are used to evaluate product direction, usability, accessibility, compatibility, performance, local storage behavior, reminders, weather mode, and release readiness.

## What to Expect

A testing build may:

- crash, freeze, display incorrectly, or perform slowly;
- contain incomplete, disabled, experimental, or changing features;
- produce incorrect hydration totals, streaks, achievements, analytics, reminders, challenge progress, translations, or weather recommendations;
- behave differently across Android, iOS, web, desktop, devices, operating system versions, screen sizes, time zones, and locales;
- require reinstalling, clearing app storage, or repeating setup;
- migrate, reset, corrupt, or permanently lose test data;
- be unavailable, withdrawn, or replaced without notice.

Do not use a testing build for safety-critical, medical, legal, financial, employment, or required recordkeeping purposes.

## Test Data

Assume test data may be lost. The current app is local-first and has no Hydrion account backup. Hydrion cannot recover information that exists only on your device.

Keep independent records of anything important and do not enter information that you cannot afford to lose.

## Health and Safety

Hydrion remains a general wellness product during testing. Test outputs are especially likely to be incomplete or inaccurate.

Do not use a test build for diagnosis, treatment, emergency decisions, fluid restriction management, clinical monitoring, medication timing, or supervision of another person's safety.

## Privacy During Testing

Hydrion handles tester information according to the Privacy Policy. A test may request optional feedback, screenshots, screen recordings, device details, logs, or reproduction steps. Hydrion should explain what is requested and why.

Before submitting feedback:

- remove passwords, credentials, financial information, government identifiers, and unrelated personal information;
- avoid private medical information unless it is necessary and an appropriate secure channel has been provided;
- remove another person's information;
- remember that public repository issues and discussions may be visible to anyone.

## Feedback

Useful feedback includes the build identifier, platform, device or simulator, operating system version, steps to reproduce, expected behavior, actual behavior, and non-sensitive evidence.

By voluntarily providing feedback, you allow Hydrion to use it for product development, testing, documentation, support, and release planning without payment or obligation. This does not allow Hydrion to use your personal information contrary to the Privacy Policy.

## Tester Conduct

Testers must not intentionally harm users, devices, local data, infrastructure, third-party providers, or project maintainers. Do not publish secrets, signing material, private security details, or another person's personal information.

## Leaving Testing

You may leave testing by uninstalling the test build, leaving the platform testing program, and clearing local app storage if desired. Because the current app has no accounts, there is no Hydrion account-deletion flow.

Questions about testing may be sent to hydrionsharks@gmail.com.
